    About the Conference

    Advancing learning that truly delivers impact

    The inaugural ACTLC Conference brings together academic leaders, faculty, and teaching and learning professionals from across the region for a gathering focused on learning for impact — advancing agency, future readiness, and purpose in higher education.

    At a time when higher education is under increasing pressure to demonstrate its relevance and societal impact, this conference positions learning at the center of transformation, focusing on how institutions can intentionally design teaching, learning, and assessment to develop future-ready graduates with agency, adaptability, and purpose.

    Conference Themes

    Four Sub-Themes, One Direction

    All proposals must align with one of the four sub-themes. Practice-led. Evidence-informed. 500–1,000 words.

    01
    Future-Ready Curricula and Graduates
    Employability, purpose, and societal impact
    • Aligning graduate attributes with workforce and societal needs
    • Curriculum renewal for relevance, adaptability, and employability
    • Co-designing curricula with employers, industry, and communities
    • Interdisciplinary and challenge-based learning
    • Micro-credentials, stackable learning, and lifelong learning pathways
    • Measuring and evidencing graduate outcomes
    02
    Assessment Reimagined for Capability and Agency
    Authentic, programmatic, and competency-based approaches
    • Real-world, performance-based, and programmatic assessment
    • Assessment that builds confidence, autonomy, and learner agency
    • Portfolios, capstones, and reflective practice
    • Rethinking grading, feedback, and progression
    • Ensuring coherence, academic standards, and progression integrity
    03
    Teaching & Learning in an AI- and Data-Rich Environment
    Innovation with purpose and integrity
    • Blended, hybrid, and digitally enhanced learning models
    • AI as a co-pilot for teaching, learning design, and assessment
    • Learning analytics and evidence-informed teaching
    • Ethical use of data and educational technologies
    • Enhancing rather than replacing human judgment
    • Faculty capability and digital pedagogy development
    04
    Personalised, Flexible, and Inclusive Learning at Scale
    Equity, support, and student success
    • Designing personalised learning experiences at institutional scale
    • Flexible learning models and pathways
    • Equity-driven personalisation and inclusive pedagogies
    • Technology-enabled advising and learner support systems
    • Supporting student success, belonging, and well-being

    Call for Best Practices

    Share What You Actually Did

    Practice-led. We are seeking proposals that showcase what was implemented, how it was done, what changed, and what others can learn.

    Important Dates

    30 October 2026
    Proposal Submission Deadline
    500–1,000 words, Word or PDF format
    15 November 2026
    Notification of Acceptance
    Authors notified of review decision
    15 December 2026
    Revised Proposals (if applicable)
    Revisions submitted if requested by committee
    10 January 2027
    Final Presentation Materials Due
    PowerPoint (.pptx), 10–12 slides, 15 min presentation
    Proposal Structure (500–1,000 words)
    • Cover page: title, sub-theme alignment, author & institution details
    • Context & challenge: institutional setting and problem addressed
    • The practice or innovation: what was designed or changed, who was involved
    • Implementation: how introduced, scope, scale, and timeline
    • Impact & evidence: outcomes, qualitative and quantitative indicators
    • Lessons learned & transferability: what other institutions can adapt
    Submission Format
    • File format: Word (.docx) or PDF
    • Length: 500–1,000 words
    • Font size: 11–12 pt, line spacing: 1.15–1.5
    • Include contact details of the corresponding author
    Presentation Format
    • 15-minute showcase presentation
    • 10–12 slides, PowerPoint (.pptx) format
    • Practice-focused and evidence-informed, not promotional
